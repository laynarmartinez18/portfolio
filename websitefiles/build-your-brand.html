// 01 — The Challenge

A New Name Needs a New Home

Harvard Online had something most brands would kill for: instant recognition. What it didn't have was a digital presence worthy of it. In October 2021, the organization made the decision to establish a dedicated online home for its growing catalogue of courses — and set an ambitious four-month deadline to get there.

That timeline was, to put it charitably, daunting. Building a website from discovery through design through development through launch in four months requires everything to go right: clear decision-making, tight stakeholder alignment, and someone who can hold the whole vision together while managing a thousand details simultaneously.

I was that person.

"Four months to build a website for Harvard. No pressure. I immediately opened a spreadsheet and got to work."

// 02 — The Build

End-to-End, No Shortcuts

I led the project from the initial discovery phase straight through to launch day. That meant managing external vendors, driving internal stakeholder reviews, making design calls under time pressure, and writing copy that could hold its own next to the Harvard name.

The course pages were the critical piece — they're where prospective learners make enrollment decisions, so every word, every layout choice, every UX pattern had to earn its place. I designed wireframes for all pages with a close eye on conversion, went through multiple rounds of feedback, and pushed hard for designs that were both intuitive and compelling.

We made the deadline. The site launched on time, and HarvardOnline.com became the centralized hub it needed to be — a brand that could grow into the institution behind it.

// 03 — Beyond the Launch

Making the Learner the Story

A strong website is table stakes. What makes a brand feel real is the people behind it — and Harvard Online's learners are genuinely remarkable. Students from every country, every background, every career stage. Each of them with a story worth telling.

I developed a process for capturing learner testimonials through video, designing a collection approach that worked across Harvard Online's global audience. Those stories didn't just live on the site — they powered email campaigns, social content, and ultimately shaped how the team thought about what courses to build next. Real feedback from real learners, turned into product insight.

I also built the templates and content systems that the team would use long after launch — brand guidelines, reusable page structures, content playbooks — so the brand could keep growing consistently without having to rebuild from scratch every time.

What This Project Required

Website strategy and architecture · UX wireframing and stakeholder review · Vendor management · Brand-aligned copywriting · Learner testimonial video program · Email and social campaign assets · Templates and brand documentation for long-term use.

// 04 — Takeaway

The Brand Was Always There. It Just Needed a Home.

The most important thing I learned working on this project is that a recognizable name is an asset and a responsibility at the same time. Every design choice, every line of copy, every UX decision either honors the brand or erodes it. You don't get to be sloppy just because the logo is famous.

What I built for Harvard Online wasn't just a website — it was a foundation. A set of systems, standards, and stories that the team could build on. That's the kind of work I care about most: not just launching something, but building something that lasts.
